DEEP C Industrial Zones


Eco-Industrial Parks in Haiphong & Quang Ninh

🏭

First established in 1997 in Haiphong City through a collaboration between the Belgian group Rent-A-Port and a Vietnamese local authority to develop the Dinh Vu Industrial Zone (now DEEP C Haiphong I). Since its inception, it has evolved into one of the largest industrial zone developers in Vietnam.

Scale & Footprint

DEEP C manages a total of five industrial zones spanning over 3,500 hectares. Distributed across two key regions: DEEP C Haiphong I, II, III, and DEEP C Quang Ninh I, II.

Strategic Location

Positioned at the center of North Vietnam’s infrastructure. Prime access to Lach Huyen Deep Sea Port (100,000 DWT), Haiphong Port, international airports, and major expressways.

Real Estate Solutions

Offers industrial land alongside ready-built workshops, warehouses, and offices (1,350m² - 5,400m²). Includes built-to-suit options with 2.5T/m² floor loading and over 9m free height.

Reliable Utilities

Robust underground networks, redundant treated water, ISO-certified wastewater treatment, underground telecom, and highly reliable power backed by ongoing renewable energy.

4.35% CIT

Tax Incentives

Investors benefit from highly competitive Economic Zone tax incentives, notably an average Corporate Income Tax of 4.35% for the first 15 years (lower than the standard 20%).

Global Community

A trusted location for over 120 investors globally across various industries. Long-term partners include multinational corporations like Chevron Lubricants and Nakashima.

Eco-Industrial Park Transition

DEEP C is transitioning into an Eco-Industrial Park (EIP) model through a comprehensive strategy emphasizing circular economy principles, international collaboration, and a dedicated governance structure. This aligns with the Global Eco-Industrial Park Standard set by UNIDO.

A Dual Approach

DEEP C is simultaneously transforming its existing zones (Haiphong 2 and 3) to comply with EIP requirements, while designing and developing new zones (Quang Ninh 1 and 2) to meet EIP standards right from their inception.

GEIPP UNIDO Program
Phase 1 (2020–2024):

DEEP C Haiphong 1 (DVIZ) was selected as a pilot zone to establish EIP foundations (industrial symbiosis & RECP practices). Successfully achieved 52 out of 64 applicable indicators under the International EIP Framework.

Phase 2 (2024–2028):

Aims to deeply integrate circular economy principles by promoting "closed-loop" systems. The waste or by-products from one company are utilized as raw inputs for another.

Industrial Symbiosis

"DEEP C Connect"

Breaking away from the linear "take-make-dispose" model, DEEP C views waste as a valuable new resource. They initiated the DEEP C Connect project acting as a bridge and data facilitator to help tenants identify and engage in resource exchange (surplus products, by-products, or waste streams).

Dedicated Governance

Expanded the Sustainability Team in 2024 to specifically oversee the EIP transition. They are responsible for assessing compliance with EIP criteria (e.g., Vietnam's Decree 35/2022/NĐ-CP), developing action plans, and leading the organization's emission reduction roadmap.

The PPP Approach

The core pillars of DEEP C's sustainability strategy are built upon the PPP approach, which balances three foundational ESG pillars: Planet, People, and Profit.

🌍

Planet

Dedicated to reducing environmental impact and fostering ecological resilience. Core focus areas include the adoption of renewable energy (wind and solar), improving energy efficiency, and carefully managing greenhouse gas emissions. Additionally, DEEP C emphasizes circular economy principles through integrated water and waste management, and invests in green infrastructure, biodiversity, and nature-based ecosystems.

🤝

People

Centers on creating a safe, inclusive, and supportive environment for employees, tenants, and local residents. This means prioritizing occupational health and safety, fostering diversity, equity, and inclusion, and providing robust training. DEEP C focuses on building local communities—such as the "DEEP C Care for Children" program—and actively engaging with tenants to build a collaborative business ecosystem.

📈

Profit

Ensures that the company's economic progress is responsible, ethical, and shared. It encompasses direct economic performance, financial integrity, and managing the positive and negative indirect economic impacts on the surrounding regions. This pillar emphasizes transparent tax practices and responsible procurement, prioritizing local and ethical sourcing to strengthen regional economies and minimize environmental footprints.

MB Ageas Life x DEEP C

A strategic partnership between MB Ageas Life and DEEP C Industrial Zones represents a powerful opportunity to elevate the "People" pillar of sustainable development. By blending DEEP C’s established infrastructure with MB Ageas Life's financial protection expertise, the two entities can forge a resilient, high-retention workforce ecosystem.

When developing a high-tech and sustainable industrial park in Vietnam, establishing a robust social safety net is just as critical as building green infrastructure. Because both DEEP C (originally a joint venture with Belgian investor Rent-A-Port ) and MB Ageas Life (backed by Belgium’s Ageas Group) share a European heritage rooted in strong ESG standards, their alignment on worker welfare is a natural fit.

Here is a blueprint for how an integrated insurance program can create a healthy, stable, and sustainable community within DEEP C’s eco-industrial parks:

1. Proactive Health and Applied Preventive Medicine

DEEP C already places a massive emphasis on Occupational Health and Safety (OHS), achieving zero work-related accidents and zero occupational diseases in 2024. They also operate a 24/7 First Aid Clinic at DEEP C Hai Phong 2 in partnership with Viet Tiep Hospital.

MB Ageas Life can build upon this foundation by shifting the focus from reactive care to proactive well-being. By integrating principles of applied preventive medicine into a group health insurance rider, the program could:

  • 🏥 Fund annual, comprehensive health screenings for workers directly at the onsite clinic.
  • 💻 Provide coverage for telemedicine consultations, nutritional counseling, and mental health workshops.
  • 📉 Offer premium discounts to tenant companies that maintain zero-accident records and high OHS compliance, incentivizing a culture of safety.
2. Family Security and Education continuity

A worker's stability is deeply tied to their family's financial security. DEEP C already demonstrates a commitment to the next generation through its "DEEP C Care for Children" project, which provides long-term financial support and mentoring to vulnerable students until they complete their education.

MB Ageas Life specializes in life insurance and education savings funds. A joint "Family Shield" initiative could guarantee that if a worker suffers a critical illness or fatal accident, MB Ageas steps in to fund the children's education. This ensures that a sudden tragedy does not force children to abandon their educational pursuits to enter the workforce prematurely.

3. "DEEP C Connect" Micro-Insurance Integration

DEEP C acts as a facilitator for its tenants through platforms like "DEEP C Connect," which builds a collaborative business ecosystem. DEEP C could negotiate a master insurance framework with MB Ageas Life, offering tenant companies access to premium group life and health insurance at heavily discounted, wholesale rates.

For Tenants:

This acts as a powerful talent retention tool, reducing turnover and training costs.

For Workers:

It provides access to high-tier financial protection (covering hospital allowances, maternity, and critical illness) that they might not be able to afford individually.

4. Financial Literacy and Long-Term Stability

Beyond infrastructure, the "Social" pillar encompasses improving the overall well-being of residents and stakeholders.

💡

The Tripartite Co-Pay Model

Shared Investment Framework

This model treats the insurance program as a joint venture between all three stakeholders, fostering a strong sense of shared responsibility and ensuring long-term sustainability.

15%

The Developer

Contributes a direct financial subsidy to the master premium. This is justified as a marketing and real estate asset; a park with heavily subsidized healthcare attracts premium tenants, allowing the developer to maintain higher land lease rates and occupancy levels.

70%

The Tenants

Carry the bulk of the premium. However, because the developer and workers are co-funding the pool, the tenants secure a much richer benefits package for their workforce than they could afford independently.

15%

The Workers

Pay a small percentage of the premium. Having "skin in the game" reduces frivolous utilization of the benefits and ensures workers place a high perceived value on the insurance program provided by their employer and the park.

Beyond Operating Expenses

Treat this not as an operating expense, but as a multi-layered financial and strategic asset for DEEP C. Here is how MB Ageas Life can detail the financial benefits to DEEP C, moving from direct cash offsets to massive ESG valuations:

1. Platform Facilitation Fees

(The "Cash Back" Angle)

Under Vietnamese insurance regulations, direct "commission" typically goes to licensed agents or brokers. However, MB Ageas Life can structure a B2B arrangement where DEEP C Management Company (DCMC) receives a Platform Facilitation or Data Management Fee.

The Mechanism

Because DEEP C is leveraging its "DEEP C Connect" platform to aggregate tenant participation, streamline marketing, and potentially facilitate payroll deductions, MB Ageas Life saves massively on customer acquisition and administrative costs.

The Financial Benefit

MB Ageas Life returns a percentage of the total annualized premium back to DEEP C as a service fee. Over a 3-to-5 year timeline, as tenant participation scales, this recurring revenue stream can completely offset DEEP C’s initial 10%–15% premium subsidy, rendering the program net-neutral or even profitable for the developer.

2. Co-Investment in Social Utilities

Instead of simply paying out claims, MB Ageas Life can pledge to reinvest a calculated percentage of its underwriting profit directly back into DEEP C’s physical footprint.

The Mechanism

MB Ageas Life establishes a "Community Health Dividend." If the overall claim rates stay below a certain threshold—due to a healthy, safe working environment—a portion of the surplus is redirected into park utilities.

The Financial Benefit

This capital can be used to dramatically expand the existing 24/7 First Aid Clinic operated with Viet Tiep Hospital. By upgrading this facility into a specialized hub for applied preventive medicine, the park can offer advanced occupational health screenings and continuous wellness monitoring. DEEP C gains state-of-the-art social infrastructure at a fraction of the capital expenditure, heavily subsidized by the insurer.

3. Amplifying ESG CSR Initiatives

(The Charity Angle)

DEEP C already dedicates significant resources to corporate social responsibility, such as the "DEEP C Care for Children" project which provides scholarships and mentoring to vulnerable students.

The Mechanism

MB Ageas Life can introduce a "Matching Impact" clause within the master framework. For every policy activated under the Tripartite Model, MB Ageas Life commits a matching donation to DEEP C’s designated community funds.

The Financial Benefit

This effectively doubles DEEP C’s charitable footprint and social impact without the developer needing to allocate additional budget. It strengthens community goodwill, local government relations, and fulfills key UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) effortlessly.

4. Enhancing High-Tech ESG Valuation

(The Magnet Effect)

While harder to measure in immediate cash flow, this is arguably the most lucrative long-term benefit for an industrial developer.

The Mechanism

The Tripartite Model generates highly structured, auditable data on worker welfare, health equity, and financial inclusion. MB Ageas Life can provide DEEP C with anonymized, comprehensive annual impact reports detailing the specific social safety net established within the park.

The Financial Benefit

A verifiable, premium social safety net is a massive competitive differentiator. It provides the exact type of rigorous, transparent ESG data that tier-one US investment and consulting firms require when deploying capital into high-tech, sustainable industrial parks. It shifts DEEP C's value proposition from merely offering "green land" to offering a fully integrated, low-risk, high-retention human capital ecosystem, allowing DEEP C to command premium lease rates and attract top-tier multinational tenants.

3. Ecosystem (Cashback)
